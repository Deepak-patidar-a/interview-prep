# Tell Me About Yourself

## Question
Walk me through your journey as a frontend engineer and what you've been working on recently.

---

## My Answer
I'm a passionate frontend engineer who loves solving real world frontend issues. Recently I'm building a full-fledged AI disease detection platform for crops with real-time expert chat, mic accessibility, and multilanguage support.

## What I Got Right
- Mentioned a real project with tangible features
- Showed initiative beyond day job with side project
- Mentioned multilingual, real-time, AI features — impressive stack

## What I Got Wrong
- Didn't mention Blue Yonder or 5+ years of experience at all
- No measurable impact — seniors lead with numbers
- "Passionate engineer who loves solving problems" is too generic — every candidate says this
- No clear career narrative — where I started, where I am, where I'm going

## Key Things to Remember

**The 90-second pitch structure:**
1. Current role + company + what you build
2. One key impact/achievement with a number
3. What you're building/learning on the side
4. What you care about / your engineering philosophy

**What interviewers are listening for:**
- Can you communicate clearly and concisely?
- Do you have real impact to show, not just responsibilities?
- Are you growing outside your job?

**Memory trick:** Role → Impact → Side project → Philosophy. RISP.

**Never say:**
- "I'm passionate about..." (everyone says this)
- Generic phrases without specifics
- Just recite your resume line by line

---

## Things to Improve
- Prepare a crisp 90-second version and practice saying it out loud
- Always lead with current role and company in the first sentence
- Have 2-3 metrics ready to drop naturally
