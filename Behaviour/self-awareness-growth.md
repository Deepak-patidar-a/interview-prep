# Self Awareness — Gap Between Now and Truly Senior

## Question
Where do you see the gap between where you are today and a truly senior frontend engineer? What are you actively working on to close it?

---

## My Answer
Currently lacking in ownership — keeping code clean proactively, seeing that "this code should be more optimized", thinking about further UX enhancements. I can take ownership but my current company isn't paying me well so I only do what a normal frontend engineer would do. I know frontend system design and can do much better with ownership.

## What I Got Right
- Self awareness about ownership gap — recognizing it is a senior trait
- Knew the gap wasn't technical — that's actually mature insight
- Honest answer

## What I Got Wrong
- "My company doesn't pay me well so I do the minimum" — this is extremely damaging in an interview
- It signals external motivation only — seniors are expected to have internal drive
- It implies "I'll do the minimum here too if I'm unhappy"
- It contradicts everything strong shown in the previous 11 answers
- Never say this. Even if it's completely true. Rephrase it.

## Key Things to Remember

**What ownership actually means at senior level:**
- Proactively identifying problems, not waiting to be told
- Caring about code quality beyond your own PRs
- Thinking about the user experience end-to-end, not just your component
- Taking responsibility for production issues even when it's not "your" code
- Driving decisions with data and documented reasoning

**How to reframe compensation frustration:**
- Never say: "I'm underpaid so I don't go above and beyond"
- Say instead: "I'm looking for an environment where I can take full ownership and have greater scope of impact"
- This says the same thing — you want more — without damaging your credibility

**The self-awareness question — what interviewers are really asking:**
- Do you know your weaknesses? (self-awareness)
- Are you actively doing something about them? (growth mindset)
- Are you honest without being self-destructive? (maturity)

**Strong answer structure:**
1. Name the gap honestly and specifically (not "I want to learn more")
2. Explain why it's a gap — what does good look like?
3. Show the concrete thing you're doing to close it
4. Tie it to something already on your resume

**What NOT to say:**
- "I want to improve my communication" — too generic
- "I need to learn more technologies" — vague, sounds insecure
- "My company limits me" — external blame, red flag
- "I don't really have major gaps" — overconfident, unbelievable

**Memory trick:** Gap → Why it matters → What I'm doing. GWD. "Got Work to Do — and here's exactly what."
